Joseph Smith's Residencies

  • Vermont (1805–1816)
  • New York (1816–1830)
  • Kirtland, Ohio (1831–1838)
  • Missouri (1831–1839)
  • Nauvoo, Illinois (1839–1844)

Joseph Smith (1805–1844) is regarded by members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints as a prophet
chosen by God to restore the gospel of Jesus Christ and re-establish Christ’s church on the earth.
Born in Vermont and raised in upstate New York, he grew up during a time of intense religious excitement known as the Second Great Awakening.

The rose that grew from concrete
Joseph Smith singlehandedly put the reawakening movement
on his incredibly broad shoulders.

Additional Accomplishments

Beyond his miraculous acts, Joseph Smith:

  • Founded The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in 1830
  • Translated the Book of Mormon
  • Introduced priesthood authority and temple ordinances

Fun Facts

- Joseph Smith reportedly had visions from a young age.
- He survived multiple assassination attempts.
- His writings and teachings continue to influence millions today.
