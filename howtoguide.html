Stock Market Basics

A simple guide to understanding ownership, trading, and how the global economy moves.

What is the Stock Market?

[Image of stock market trading floor with screens]

At its core, the stock market is a marketplace where buyers and sellers come together to trade shares of publicly held companies. Think of it like a supermarket, but instead of buying groceries, you are buying tiny pieces of ownership in companies like Apple, Tesla, or Coca-Cola.

When you buy a "share" or "stock," you become a partial owner of that company. If the company does well, the value of your share typically goes up. If the company struggles, the value goes down.

How It Works

01

IPO (Initial Public Offering)

A private company decides it needs capital to grow. It issues shares to the public for the first time. This is the primary market.

02

The Exchange

Once shares are issued, they are traded on stock exchanges like the NYSE (New York Stock Exchange) or Nasdaq. This is the secondary market, where most investing happens.

03

Supply & Demand

Stock prices change constantly during the day. If more people want to buy a stock than sell it, the price goes up. If more people want to sell than buy, the price drops.

Common Terminology

Master these terms to navigate financial news with confidence.

↑

Bull Market

Prices are rising. Represents optimism and economic growth.

↓

Bear Market

Prices fall 20% or more. Represents pessimism and slowdown.

$

Dividend

A portion of profits paid directly to shareholders as cash.

#

Market Cap

The total value of a company (Share Price x Total Shares).

%

Volatility

How rapidly prices change. High volatility = higher risk.

⊞

ETF

Exchange Traded Fund. A basket of many stocks bought as one.
